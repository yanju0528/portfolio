POLITECNICO DI MILANO

After World War II, the French architect Fernand Pouillon stated, "It is enough to have one Corbusier in the world, and I will dedicate myself to designing architecture for civilians." This statement continues to influence my thinking, as it challenges me to reconsider architecture not merely as form-making or grand narratives, but as something grounded in everyday life.
